01
Vinyl
Polyvinyl Chloride · 12" · 10" · 7"

The dominant format from the 1950s through the 1980s and a strong revival since the 2000s. Vinyl's large format — particularly the 12" LP — gave designers the most canvas to work with of any physical music format. Single sleeve, gatefold, tip-on, and die-cut variants each have distinct spec requirements.

02
CD
Compact Disc · Jewel Case · Digipak · Slim Case

Dominant from the mid-1980s through the 2000s. The CD booklet — printed on coated paper, folded into a jewel case — became the primary canvas for detailed liner notes, photography, and typography. Digipak and slim case variants offer different structural possibilities.

03
Cassette
Compact Cassette · J-Card · O-Card

The cassette's J-card — named for the J-shape of its fold — is one of the most constrained design challenges in physical music packaging. The front panel, spine, and back must all be read at once. A revival since the 2010s has brought new packaging creativity to the format.

04
Box Set
Multi-Disc · Collector's Editions · Slip Cases

Box sets represent the most complex packaging design challenge — multiple formats, components, and inserts all needing to cohere visually within a single outer box. Slip cases, clamshells, and rigid lift-lid boxes each have different structural and print requirements.

Sub-formats

Slip Case
Open-Ended Outer Sleeve · Contents Slide In
General Specifications 01
Key principleAll box set dimensions are custom — there is no standard. Dimensions are determined by the contents (number of LPs, CDs, books, etc.). Always build a physical mock-up before going to print.
Board Weight2–3mm grey board wrapped with printed sheet
Typical LP Slip CaseApprox 325 × 325 × varies mm
Typical CD Slip CaseApprox 143 × 125 × varies mm
Print Resolution300 dpi · CMYK
Design Notes 02
Wrap allowanceAdd 15–20mm wrap-around on all edges for gluing. This area should be plain or extend your background colour — it won't be visible but affects registration.
FinishMatte laminate most common on slip cases — reduces slip and fingerprinting. Foil stamping on box sets reads as premium. Embossing on the outer box is extremely effective.
Clamshell
Hinged Box · Opens Like a Book
Construction 01
StructureTwo rigid boards joined by a cloth or paper hinge spine
Board2.5–3mm grey board · wrapped or case-bound
NoteClamshells require a specialist bindery. The hinge spine needs to flex thousands of times. Specify cloth hinge for longevity. All dimensions custom — provide exact contents list to manufacturer.
Lift-Lid Box
Rigid Base + Separate Lid · Trophy Format
Construction 01
StructureSeparate lid and base — lid fits over base with friction
Board2–3mm rigid grey board · cloth or paper wrapped
Lid ClearanceAdd 2–3mm to base dimensions for lid fit
Design NoteLift-lid boxes are the most premium box set format. Often used for super-deluxe editions. The lid top is the primary canvas — large, flat, uninterrupted. Foil stamp or deboss here for maximum impact.
